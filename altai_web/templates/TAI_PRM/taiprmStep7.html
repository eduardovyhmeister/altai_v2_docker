Likelihood Classification

A likelihood or occurrence ranking metric helps measure how frequently an analysed failure mode could occur. The probability of occurrence (Pf) should be based on the failure mode's probability of occurring during operation time. The time frame's homogenisation should be based on an hourly or a 1E-6 hourly base for each failure mode considered. This designation of time frame is used since its commonly used also in criticality analyses. In case the process is sporadic, The last of the following tables could be used. Even though this distinction could obtain different rankings, as long as homogenization is performed for each classification, the shift in ranking would be homogenized in the end analysis. The first of the following tables shows the ranking and occurrence level in function of the description or designation of the occurrence. A critical characteristic of the occurrences ratios for some intrinsic algorithmic processes is that confusion matrixes estimations could estimate them. These numbers could be used directly for specific robustness-based failure modes. We, ASSISTANT, recommend its use if readily available. This approach is considered within the pipeline process of the proposed framework.

Occurrence Ranking Criteria Likelihood or Level of Occurrence in function of probabilities as a single failure mode

Occurrence description of the Failure mode Severity Rank Failure severity Classification
Once a week. High probability is defined as a single Pf > 0.20 of the overall probability of failure during the item operating interval. 10 High Probability (Level A or I)
Once every two weeks. Probability is defined as a single Pf > 0.10 but 0.20 > Pf of the overall probability of failure during the item operating time interval. 7-9 Probable (Level B or II)
Once a month. Occasional is defined as a single Pf > 0.01 but 0.1 > Pf of the overall probability of failure during the item operating time interval 4-6 Occasional (Level C or III)
Once every two months. Remote is defined as a single 0.01 > Pf > 0.001 of the overall probability of failure during the item operating time interval. 2-3 Remote (Level D or IV)
An unlikely probability of occurrence during the item operating time interval. Unlikely is defined as a single 0.001 > Pf of the overall probability of failure during the item operating time interval. 1 Unlikely (Level E or V)

Each occurrence is considered during the system/part/sub-part/component operating time or the ratio of item build with failing conditions during the operating time. The normalisation comes from the operating time specification (e.g. hourly or 1E-6 hourly).
